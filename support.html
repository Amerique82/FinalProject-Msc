Accounts & Access

Visit NEST and sign in with your university username (your student ID) and the temporary password sent to your personal email on enrolment. You'll be prompted to change your password and set up MFA on first login.
Go to Microsoft Self-Service Password Reset and enter your @roehampton.ac.uk email address. You'll need access to your personal email or mobile phone for the verification code. If you're locked out completely, contact the ServiceDesk with your student ID card.
Download the Microsoft Authenticator app on your phone (iOS or Android). Then sign into your Roehampton account at mysignins.microsoft.com and follow the prompts to add the Authenticator app as your verification method. MFA is mandatory for all university systems.

Wi-Fi & Network

Select the eduroam network on your device. When prompted, enter your full @roehampton.ac.uk email address as the username and your university password. On Windows, you may need to accept the certificate from roehampton.ac.uk. Download the eduroam configuration assistant for automatic setup.
Download Cisco AnyConnect from NEST Software & Apps. Install it, then connect to vpn.roehampton.ac.uk using your university credentials. VPN gives you access to library databases, shared drives and systems that are only available on campus.
First, try forgetting the eduroam network and reconnecting. If the problem persists in a specific room, please report it to the ServiceDesk with the room number and time of the issue so IT can investigate the access point. Temporary issues can also be caused by high density of devices — try moving to a different area.

Software & Apps

Computing students have access to: Microsoft 365 (Word, Excel, PowerPoint, Teams, OneDrive), GitHub Copilot, JetBrains IDEs (IntelliJ, PyCharm, WebStorm, etc.), MATLAB, Adobe Creative Cloud (selected apps), Cisco Packet Tracer, and more. Log in to NEST Software & Apps to see the full catalogue.
Go to education.github.com and sign in with your GitHub account. Click "Get student benefits" and verify your enrolment using your @roehampton.ac.uk email. Approval typically takes 1–3 days. Once approved, GitHub Copilot will be free in VS Code, JetBrains, and the GitHub web editor.
Yes — most licences in the Software Catalogue allow installation on personal devices. Microsoft 365 can be installed on up to 5 devices. JetBrains IDEs can be installed on any personal machine using your student licence. Some software (e.g., MATLAB campus licence) is limited to university-managed devices — the catalogue clearly indicates device restrictions for each title.

Moodle & Learning

Modules are typically added to Moodle within 48 hours of the start of term. If a module is still missing after the first week of teaching, first check that you are correctly enrolled on the module via NEST. If enrolment is confirmed but the module is still absent, contact the Computing Department — it may need to be manually added.
Go to your module on Moodle and find the assignment submission link. Click it, then upload your file(s) and click Save changes. You'll receive a confirmation email to your Roehampton address. Always submit before the deadline shown — late submissions may be penalised per the university's late submission policy.
