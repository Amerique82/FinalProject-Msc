About the Computing Department

Who we are, what we do, and how we support the Roehampton Computing community.

Mission

Empowering the Roehampton Computing community through technology.

The Computing Department Information Hub exists to ensure every student, academic, and member of staff has seamless access to the tools, services and knowledge they need to learn, teach, and conduct research. We believe that excellent digital infrastructure is the foundation of excellent education.

Vision

A digitally inclusive community

By 2028, every member of the Roehampton Computing community has frictionless access to world-class digital services — regardless of technical background, ability, or location. Our portal is the single front door to everything digital.

What is this portal?

Why the Computing Hub exists

This portal was created after research with Computing staff and students revealed a fragmented digital experience — services were scattered, hard to find, and not tailored to user roles. The Hub centralises access, organises services by role, and provides clear guidance to reduce IT friction.

Our academic area

Part of the School of Computing, Engineering and the Built Environment

At CEBE you join a connected, inclusive community where innovation meets opportunity — professionally focused education designed alongside industry partners, with strong graduate outcomes and clear pathways to employment.

Where you will learn: the Sir David Bell Building

Computing students study in our media centre, working with the same software and platforms used in industry:

  • Six dedicated Computing Labs
  • A dedicated Cyber Security Lab
  • A Mac computer suite
  • Professional tools throughout — Python, Java, Git, cloud platforms and machine learning frameworks

Hands-on, industry-connected education

From Software Development to Data Science, Cyber Security and emerging digital technologies, our courses prepare you for the future of work through practical, employer-led projects and interdisciplinary collaboration.

Our research and knowledge exchange has civic and global impact — driving solutions to the challenges of tomorrow.

Our values

How we work

Accessibility first

Everything we build meets WCAG 2.1 AA standards. Digital inclusion is non-negotiable.

Security by design

Data protection and cybersecurity are built into every service, not bolted on afterwards.

User-centred

Services are designed based on research with real students and staff — not assumptions.

Sustainable IT

We favour energy-efficient infrastructure and responsible procurement across all operations.

Open and transparent

Clear communication about service changes, outages and new features — no surprises.

Continuous improvement

We collect feedback, measure outcomes, and iterate — the Hub evolves with the community.

Our functions

What the Computing Hub delivers

Six core functions underpin everything we do for the Roehampton Computing community.

1

Computing & Software Services

Provisioning and support for all software, licences, lab equipment and digital workspaces used in teaching and learning.

2

Network & Connectivity

Managing campus Wi-Fi, VPN, wired networking and connectivity for students, staff and research systems.

3

Learning Technology

Supporting the VLE (Moodle), lecture capture (Panopto), accessibility tools and innovative digital learning initiatives.

4

Research Computing

Providing HPC resources, research data management, specialist software and compliance support for academic research.

5

Information Security

Cybersecurity awareness, incident response, data protection compliance and secure-by-design service delivery.

6

Digital Transformation

Leading departmental digitisation projects, improving administrative systems and piloting emerging technologies.

Find us

Where we are & how to get here

Computing Department · Digby Stuart Building
University of Roehampton · Roehampton Lane · London SW15 5PH

By rail & underground

Barnes station (National Rail) is a 20-minute walk or a short bus ride. From central London, take the District line to Putney Bridge or Hammersmith, then bus 72 or 265.

By bus

Routes 72, 85, 170, 265 and 493 stop on Roehampton Lane, directly outside the main campus entrance. Allow 30–45 minutes from central London.

By car & bicycle

Limited visitor parking is available — book ahead via reception. Secure cycle racks are located next to the Digby Stuart Building.
