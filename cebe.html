History of our School

From Victorian colleges to a school built for the digital age

One timeline, four milestones — how CEBE came to be.

  • 1841

    Our roots take hold

    Whitelands College is founded — the first of the four historic colleges (with Digby Stuart, Southlands and Froebel) that would one day form the University of Roehampton, making us one of the oldest higher-education communities in the UK.

  • 2004

    University of Roehampton

    Roehampton is granted full university status — London's campus university, combining historic grounds with modern teaching.

  • 2022

    The School of Computing, Engineering and the Built Environment

    CEBE is created to bring Computing and Sustainable Engineering and Technology together in one connected, industry-facing school — new programmes, new partnerships, new energy.

  • 2025

    The Dorothy Buchanan Building opens

    A brand-new home for Sustainable Engineering and Technology — named after the first female member of the Institution of Civil Engineers — joining the Sir David Bell Building, home of Computing.

New · Opened 2025

The Dorothy Buchanan Building

Sustainable Engineering and Technology students learn in this brand-new space. Your learning takes place in:

  • Project Studios
  • Our Digital Lab
  • Our Environmental Lab
  • Our Engineering and Fabrication Workshop
  • Our dedicated CAD Lab

There are also plenty of break-out spaces and common areas for seminars, group work and collaboration.

Computing · Media Centre

The Sir David Bell Building

Computing students study in our media centre, learning the software and platforms you will use in your future career:

  • Six dedicated Computing Labs
  • A dedicated Cyber Security Lab
  • Mac computer suite
  • Access to professional tools — Python, Java, Git, cloud platforms and machine learning frameworks
